A collection of call-and-response attention getters. Pick one and use it whenever we need the class's eyes and ears. Say the call out loud -- students give the response and follow along with the action.

## Hands on Top

Say **"Hands on top!"** while putting your hands flat on your head.

Students copy you and reply: **"That means stop!"** -- hands on heads, mouths closed, eyes on you.

## Big Boulders

Say each line out loud while doing the action -- students join in and follow along.

- **Hands on your shoulders** – "Big boulders!"
- **Hands on your knees** – "Buzzy buzzy bees!"
- **Hands on your chin** – "Let's begin!"
- **Hands on your chest** – "You're the best!"
- **Hands on your tummy** – "Something funny!"
- **Hands on your hair** – "Almost there!"
- **Hands on your feet** – "What a treat!"
- **Hands on your back** – "How are you Jack! Haven't seen you for ears and ears, but I still nose you!"

Repeat until everyone is settled and focused on you.

## More calls and responses

- **"One, two, three, eyes on me!"** – "One, two, eyes on you!"
- **"Macaroni cheese!"** – "Everybody freeze!"
- **"Class, class!"** – "Yes, yes!"
- **"Are you ready to listen?"** – "Yes we are, loud and clear!"
- Clap a rhythm – students clap it back to you.
